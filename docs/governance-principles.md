# X-Ops Alliance Governance Principles

## Purpose

X-Ops Alliance exists to coordinate and amplify independent technical communities, initiatives and conferences without centralising power or diluting identities.

## Foundational principles

### 1. Stability

The alliance should be useful beyond a single event, person or sponsor.

Operational meaning:

- Keep governance lightweight.
- Document decisions.
- Avoid personality-driven ownership.
- Preserve continuity through simple processes.

### 2. Technical judgement

Decisions should be based on ecosystem value, practical impact and community trust.

Operational meaning:

- Avoid hype-led initiatives.
- Prioritise useful coordination.
- Respect technical diversity.
- Give space to communities with proven contribution.

### 3. Coordinated action

The alliance should turn alignment into concrete outcomes.

Operational meaning:

- Shared calendar.
- Speaker exchange.
- Cross-promotion.
- Joint reports or ecosystem maps.
- Annual coordination cycle.

## Actor types

### Member Communities

Active communities with regular activity, identity and leadership.

They may:

- Participate in coordination cycles.
- Join working groups.
- Be listed as member communities.
- Propose shared initiatives.

### Conference Partners

Events with technical relevance and an audience around them.

They may:

- Coordinate calendars.
- Amplify ecosystem messages.
- Invite member communities.
- Participate in joint visibility actions.

They do not govern the alliance by default.

### Initiative Partners

Platforms, newsletters, educational projects, tools or ecosystem initiatives.

They may:

- Provide infrastructure.
- Support visibility.
- Contribute resources.
- Help document the ecosystem.

## Facilitation team

The initial facilitation team may be operated by HackerDreams or trusted contributors.

Its role:

- Convene.
- Document.
- Maintain the website and shared assets.
- Facilitate meetings.
- Keep momentum.

Its role is not:

- Owning the alliance.
- Deciding unilaterally for members.
- Turning the alliance into a commercial funnel.
- Replacing community governance.

## Neutrality rules

The alliance must remain:

- Vendor-neutral.
- Community-respecting.
- Event-respecting.
- Non-exclusive.
- Clear about commercial boundaries.

Commercial activity can exist around the wider X-Ops ecosystem, but the alliance itself must be perceived as neutral and member-first.

## Decision rule

For the initial phase, decisions should follow this pattern:

1. Facilitation team drafts a proposal.
2. Founding members review it.
3. Objections are discussed.
4. Only lightweight consensus is required for non-critical actions.
5. Identity, membership and neutrality decisions require explicit agreement from founding members.

## Golden rule

If the alliance appears to belong to one organisation, it loses legitimacy.

If it appears to be useful to all members, it scales.
